Frontier Airlines Contract of Carriage

## 1 - Introduction
The following terms and conditions as well as such additional terms and conditions presented on Frontier Airlines' website, fare rules, published schedules or printed on or in any ticket or ticket-less travel authorization apply to all tickets issued for travel on flights operated by or for Frontier Airlines, Inc. ("Frontier"), as well as that transportation, regardless of whether such ticket was sold by Frontier or its authorized agents or whether such ticket is used ("Contract of Carriage").

## 2 - Definitions
A. Codeshare - A marketing and business arrangement in which two airlines "share" the same flight (which might include connecting legs). One airline places its designator code and flight number on a flight operated by the other airline, and markets and sells tickets for that shared flight as part of its published schedule.
E. Fare Rules - The rules and requirements associated with a ticket.
G. No-Show Cancellation - The automatic cancellation of a passenger's ticket upon such passenger failing to either (i) check-in for such passenger's flight, or (ii) board such passenger's flight, in either instance within the required times. The automatic cancellation will apply to all subsequent flights, including return flights, on the itinerary. Presentation of a ticket by someone other than the named passenger renders the ticket void and the ticket will then be treated as a No-Show Cancellation for all purposes of this Contract of Carriage.
H. Qualified Individual with a Disability - An individual with a disability who: (i) has a physical or mental impairment that, on a permanent or temporary basis, substantially limits one or more major life activities; (ii) has a record of such an impairment; or, (iii) is regarded as having such an impairment, as further defined in 14 CFR 382.5.
I. Standby Passenger - A passenger boarded subject to availability of seat space at departure time and only after all passengers having confirmed reservations for the flight have been boarded.
K. Ticket - The record of agreement, including electronic tickets, for passenger air transportation provided by the airline under certain terms and conditions to the passenger as described on the ticket, in the fare rules, and in this Contract of Carriage.

## 3 - Refusal to Transport and Special Conditions
A. Frontier may refuse to provide transportation to any person and may require that a passenger leave an aircraft or be removed from an aircraft for the following reasons, in which case Frontier will provide a refund to the original form of payment of the amount paid for their ticket, which will be the limit of Frontier's liability:
1. Government Request
2. No Seat for Safety Assistant

B. Frontier may refuse to provide transportation for the following reasons, in which case no refund to the original form of payment will be due:
1. Government Direction
2. Identification - The passenger refuses to produce a government-issued identification.
3. MyFrontier Account; Card on File - The passenger refuses to create a MyFrontier account or have a credit or debit card on file with Frontier.
4. Balance Due
5. Passports/Visas
6. Failure to Check In or Appear
7. Special Medical Requirements
8. Qualified Individual with a Disability
9. Prisoners - Frontier will not transport any prisoners under any circumstances.
10. Proper Attire - Any passenger who is barefoot and over 3 years of age, or who is not otherwise fully clothed.
11. Malodorous Condition
12. Intoxication
13. Communicable Disease or Infection
14. Refusal or Inability to Sit
15. Failure to Follow Instructions
16. Use of Ticket Issued to Other Person
17. Interference
18. Smoking/Vaping
19. Weapon
20. Purchase in Violation of Contract of Carriage

C. Refusal to Sell Transportation - Frontier may refuse to sell transportation to any person.

D. Customer of Size - If, in Frontier's sole judgment, a passenger is unable to sit in an aircraft seat without lifting either or both armrests and occupying all or a portion of the adjacent seats, or encroaching into the aisle or adjacent seats, the passenger will be required to purchase a ticket for an additional seat at the price then applicable. If sufficient, contiguous seats are not available, the passenger will be given the option to switch to flights on which such seats are available or be given a refund.

E. Allergies (Peanut, Pet, or Chemical) - Items are not removed from the aircraft to accommodate a passenger's allergy. A "peanut-free" or "chemical-free" environment cannot be provided.

H. Diversion While in Flight or Return to Gate - In the event that Frontier is required to divert an aircraft while in flight or return to gate because a passenger requires medical attention or due to the passenger's conduct, the passenger may be required to reimburse Frontier for the costs that Frontier incurs.

## 8 - Tickets
A. A passenger is entitled to transportation only upon presentation of a valid electronic ticket (e-ticket). Paper tickets are not issued on Frontier ticket stock.
B. Tickets are honored only in the order in which they are issued.
C. Prohibited practices: Back to Back Ticketing, Throwaway Ticketing, Hidden City/Point Beyond Ticketing.
F. A ticket may only be used by the person named on the ticket. Tickets may not be purchased for the transport of items of baggage.

## 9 - Ticket Validity and Itinerary Changes
A. Period of Validity
1. Tickets issued by Frontier are valid for transportation only on the flights and dates shown on the ticket. If a passenger cancels a ticket before the scheduled flight departure time, the value of the ticket less any applicable cancellation fee and certain other carrier charges will be retained for 365 days from the date of cancellation of the ticket in the form of a refund travel credit. The refund travel credit has no cash value and may only be applied to subsequent tickets on Frontier flights for the same passenger.
2. Except as required by law, Frontier shall have no obligation to reschedule passengers who cancel a ticket or to provide refund or credit for unused tickets.

B. All tickets may be canceled within twenty-four (24) hours of the purchase and a full refund to the original form of payment will be given, except for tickets purchased for travel within 7 days (168 hours) of purchase. After that time, all tickets and optional charges are non-refundable to the original form of payment unless purchased as refundable or entitled to a refund pursuant to applicable law.

## 11 - Fares
A. Fares apply for transportation only between the airports for which they are published.
C. Fares are subject to change without notice until a ticket is issued.

## 12 - Checked Baggage
A. Fees applicable to checked baggage:
1. Baggage fees apply to each checked bag.
2. Active U.S. military personnel and their accompanying spouse and children may check two bags at no charge. Overweight and oversize charges for the first two free bags are also waived.

B. Baggage Allowance Exceptions: Medical Assistive Devices, Wheelchairs, Essential Infant/Child Items - checked at no charge and do not count toward baggage allowance.

C. Acceptable Baggage:
1. Standard baggage size: maximum of 62 inches (157 cm) in linear dimension. Standard baggage weight: maximum of 40 pounds (18.1 kilograms).
2. Baggage weighing 100 or more pounds (45 kilograms) or exceeding 110 linear inches will not be accepted, with the exception of assistive devices or musical instruments.

## 17 - Failure to Operate on Schedule or Failure to Carry
A. Liability Limited - Frontier will use reasonable efforts to transport passengers and baggage to the purchased destination, but published schedules, flight times, aircraft types, seat assignments, and similar details are not guaranteed and form no part of this Contract of Carriage.

B. Force Majeure - In the occurrence of a force majeure event, Frontier may cancel, divert, or delay any flight without liability except to provide a refund for the unused portion of the ticket.

C. Delay, Misconnection, or Cancellation - In the event a passenger's flight is canceled or significantly delayed or changed, Frontier will provide transportation on its own flights at no additional charge. Frontier will have no obligation to provide transportation on another carrier. Frontier may also offer a voucher, travel credit, or other form of compensation as an alternative to a refund. If the passenger does not accept alternative transportation, Frontier will provide a refund for the unused portion of the passenger's ticket including taxes and fees for ancillary services not provided.

D. Involuntary reroute (diversion) - Frontier treats certain city groups as equivalent destinations (e.g., BWI/IAD/DCA/PHL; EWR/LGA/JFK; LAX/BUR/ONT/SNA/LGB; OAK/SFO/SJC; ORD/MDW/MKE). If diverted within a city group, Frontier has met its transport obligation and will issue no refund.

E. Schedule Change Prior to Day of Travel - When a passenger's itinerary is changed due to Frontier schedule modification and is significantly delayed or changed, Frontier shall provide a full refund for the unused portion of the ticket unless the passenger accepts the changed flight or a voucher.

F. Extended Onboard Ground Delays - Frontier maintains a separate Contingency Plan for Lengthy Tarmac Delays per FAA regulations.

## 18 - Denied Boarding Compensation
A. Voluntary - Passengers on an overbooked flight will be encouraged to voluntarily relinquish seats in exchange for alternate travel and Electronic Travel Voucher for future Frontier transportation valid 365 days.

B. Involuntary - If insufficient volunteers, passengers denied boarding will be provided transportation on next available Frontier flight. After boarding pass is scanned and passenger has boarded, removal is only for safety/security reasons.

C. Amount of Compensation:
- Domestic: New arrival within :59 = No Compensation; 1-1:59 hours = 200% (2x) one-way fare, max $1550; 2+ hours = 400% (4x) one-way fare, max $2150.
- International: New arrival within :59 = No Compensation; 1-3:59 hours = 200% (2x) one-way fare, max $1550; 4+ hours = 400% (4x) one-way fare, max $2150.

## 19 - Refunds; No-Show Cancellations and Service Charges
A. General provisions:
1. All refunds subject to government laws of the country where ticket was purchased/refund is made.
2. First portion refunded is full amount of government-imposed taxes and fees.
4. No Use: refund equals fare plus ancillary purchases plus all taxes/fees.
   Partial Use: refund based on unused portion at lowest one-way fare for class of service paid.

B. Non-No-Show situations:
1. Refundable tickets: passengers should fill out online request at www.FlyFrontier.com.
2. Tickets canceled within 24 hours of purchase (excluding those purchased within 7 days before travel): cancel online.

C. No-Show Cancellations:
1. Automatic Refund with automatic imposition of No-Show Cancellation Service Charge.
2. The Service Charge equals the fare plus all ancillary purchases plus taxes/fees.

## 20 - Currency and Mode of Payment
A. Fares in US dollars (Canadian dollars for Canadian bookings).
B. All amounts due must be paid by credit or debit card; Frontier does not accept cash except at certain international locations.

## 21 - Miscellaneous
C. Change Without Notice - Frontier reserves the right to amend this Contract without notice, provided no change applies to carriage already commenced.
I. No Class Action - Any case may be brought only in individual capacity, not as class or representative proceeding.
J. Time Limit for Action - No legal action may be brought unless commenced within 6 months from the date of the alleged incident.
K. Choice of Law - Governed by laws of the United States and the State of Colorado. All right to trial by jury is irrevocably waived.
